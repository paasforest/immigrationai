# First Customer Onboarding Guide

## Who to Target First

### Ideal First Customer Profile

**Target**: Small immigration consultancy in Lagos or Nairobi
- **Size**: 1-3 people
- **Current State**: Managing cases in WhatsApp groups and Excel
- **Pain Point**: Enough clients to feel disorganization, but not so many they need enterprise features

**Where to Find Them**:
- LinkedIn (search: "immigration consultant Lagos", "visa consultant Nairobi")
- Nigerian Bar Association immigration groups
- Immigration Facebook groups
- Local business directories
- Referrals from existing contacts

### Why This Profile?

- Small enough to onboard personally
- Large enough to see value immediately
- Active enough to provide feedback
- Willing to try new tools (early adopters)

## Handling the Payment Conversation

### Don't Wait for the Email

**On Day 10 of trial**, send a personal WhatsApp or email:

> "Hi [Name],
> 
> How has the trial been going? I noticed you've created [X] cases and generated [X] checklists. 
> 
> Happy to answer any questions before your trial ends on [date]. 
> 
> You can upgrade at [link] â€” the Professional plan at R699/month is the most popular for consultancies your size.
> 
> Let me know if you'd like to hop on a quick call to discuss!"

**Why This Works**:
- Personal outreach (not automated)
- Shows you're paying attention (usage data)
- Warm conversation (not cold renewal notice)
- Gives them time to decide (4 days before expiry)

### Pricing Objection Handling

**Most Common Objection**: "R699 is a lot."

**Your Response**:
> "How much do you charge per visa application?"

**They'll say**: R3,000 - R8,000

**Your Response**:
> "One successful application pays for 4-11 months of the platform. And if better document management helps you process even one more application per month, it pays for itself many times over."

**Alternative Response** (if they're price-sensitive):
> "I understand. The Starter plan at R299/month gives you 10 cases per month â€” that might be a better fit to start. You can always upgrade later."

## Red Flags to Watch For

### They Won't Convert If:

1. **They don't create a real case in first week**
   - They're not engaged
   - Follow up: "I noticed you haven't created a case yet. Want to do it together?"

2. **They only use one feature**
   - They're not seeing full value
   - Show them other features: "Have you tried the AI checklist? It's our most popular feature."

3. **They complain about price before using it**
   - They're not seeing ROI
   - Show them usage: "You've created 8 cases â€” that's R87.50 per case. How much do you charge per application?"

4. **They ask for features that don't exist**
   - They have specific needs
   - Document the request, but don't promise: "That's a great idea. Let me add it to our roadmap."

## What to Build Next (After First Paying Customers)

Once you have 5 paying agencies, feedback will cluster around themes. Based on this market, expect these top requests:

### 1. Mobile App for Professionals
**Why**: They work on phones. A React Native wrapper or well-optimized PWA should come before any other major feature.

**Priority**: High

### 2. WhatsApp Integration
**Why**: This market runs on WhatsApp. Being able to send case updates and document requests via WhatsApp rather than email will dramatically improve client engagement rates.

**Priority**: High

### 3. Bulk Document Requests
**Why**: Professionals want to select 5 checklist items and send one message to the client requesting all of them at once rather than triggering individual emails.

**Priority**: Medium

### 4. Application Form Assistance
**Why**: The next logical AI feature after checklists. Pre-fill UK visa application forms, Canadian Express Entry profiles, or DS-160 forms from the case data you already have.

**Priority**: Medium
